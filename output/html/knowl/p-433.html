The number \(e\) is one of the most important mathematical constants in many applications of mathematics. The number \(e\) is an irrational number, meaning it is a non-terminating and non-repeating decimal. It was discovered by Jacob Bernoulli, despite being named after Euler. The number \(e\) is a discovered constant in the universe, similar to \(\pi\text{.}\) There are multiple equivalent definitions of \(e\text{.}\) One intuitive definition comes from exponential growth, compound interest, and a particular limit.
