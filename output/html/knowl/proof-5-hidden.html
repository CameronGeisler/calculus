If \(y = x^{p/q}\text{,}\) then \(y^q = x^p\text{.}\) Then, using implicit differentiation,
\begin{equation*} \frac{d}{dx} y^q \amp = \frac{d}{dx} x^p \end{equation*}
By the power rule (for integer powers),
\begin{equation*} qy^{q-1} \frac{dy}{dx} \amp = px^{p-1} \end{equation*}
Then, solving for \(\frac{dy}{dx}\text{,}\)
\begin{equation*} \frac{dy}{dx} = \frac{px^{p-1}}{qy^{q-1}} \end{equation*}
Finally, substituting back \(y = x^{p/q}\text{,}\)
\begin{align*} \frac{dy}{dx} \amp = \frac{px^{p-1}}{q(x^{p/q})^{q-1}}\\ \amp = \frac{p}{q} \frac{x^{p-1}}{x^{p-p/q}}\\ \amp = \frac{p}{q} x^{p/q-1} \amp\amp \text{laws of exponents} \end{align*}
