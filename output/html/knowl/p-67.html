Let \(f\) be continuous at \(a\text{,}\) such that the limit,

\begin{equation*} \boxed{\lim_{h \to 0} \frac{f(x_0 + h) - f(x_0)}{h} = m} \end{equation*}

exists. Then, the tangent line to \(f\) at \(a\) is the line with slope \(m\) and that passes through the point \((x_0, f(x_0))\text{.}\) An equation of this tangent line is,

\begin{equation*} \boxed{y = m(x - x_0) + f(x_0)} \end{equation*}

We sometimes also say that the line is tangent to \(f\) at \(x_0\text{.}\)
