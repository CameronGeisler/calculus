Intuitively, the tangent line to a curve at a point passes through that point, and has the same slope, steepness, or “direction” as the curve, at the point of tangency. For example, consider this Desmos applet 1 . The word tangent comes from the Latin word for “touching”, intuitively because a tangent touches the curve at a single point, barely grazing it, instead of cutting across a curve in two places.
