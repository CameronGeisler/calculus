Theorem 4.2.6. First derivative test.

Let \(f\) be a continuous function, \(a\) be a critical point of \(f\text{.}\) Then,
  1. If \(f'\) changes from positive to negative at \(a\text{,}\) then \(f\) has a local maximum at \(a\text{.}\)
  2. If \(f'\) changes from negative to positive at \(a\text{,}\) then \(f\) has a local minimum at \(a\text{.}\)
  3. If \(f'\) is positive both to the left and right at \(a\text{,}\) or is negative both to the left and right at \(a\text{,}\) then \(f\) has neither a local minimum nor a local maximum at \(a\text{.}\)
