Proof

\begin{align*} f'(x) \amp = \lim_{h \to 0} \frac{m(x + h) + b - (mx + b)}{h}\\ \amp = \lim_{h \to 0} \frac{mh}{h}\\ \amp = \lim_{h \to 0} m\\ \amp = m \end{align*}
Again, here, the difference quotient simplifies to the constant \(m\text{,}\) because the slope does not depend on \(h\text{.}\)
