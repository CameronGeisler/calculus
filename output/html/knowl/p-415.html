Let \(f\) be \((n+1)\)-times differentiable on an interval containing \(a\) and \(x\text{.}\) Then, the error term is given by,
\begin{equation*} E_n(x) = \frac{f^{(n+1)}(c)}{(n+1)!} (x - a)^{n+1} \end{equation*}
for some \(c\) between \(a\) and \(x\text{.}\) More explicitly,
\begin{equation*} \boxed{f(x) = \underbrace{f(a) + f'(a)(x - a) + \dots + \frac{f^{(n+1)}(a)}{n!}(x - a)^n}_{T_n(x)} + \underbrace{\frac{f^{(n+1)}(c)}{(n + 1)!}(x - a)^{n+1}}_{E_n(x)}} \end{equation*}
This equation is called Taylor's formula.
