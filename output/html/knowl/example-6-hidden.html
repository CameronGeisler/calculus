Consider the derivative of \(f(x) = \frac{1}{x}\text{,}\)

\begin{align*} f'(x) \amp = \lim_{h \to 0} \frac{f(x + h) - f(x)}{h}\\ \amp = \lim_{h \to 0} \frac{\frac{1}{x + h} - \frac{1}{x}}{h} \end{align*}

To simplify this difference quotient, we first write this complex fraction as a single fraction, by multiplying numerator and denominator by \(x(x+h)\text{,}\)

\begin{align*} \amp = \lim_{h \to 0} \frac{x - (x + h)}{hx(x + h)}\\ \amp = \lim_{h \to 0} -\frac{h}{hx(x + h)}\\ \amp = \lim_{h \to 0} -\frac{1}{x(x + h)}\\ \amp = -\frac{1}{x^2} \end{align*}

In particular, notice that \(f'(x) \le 0\) for all \(x \neq 0\text{,}\) and as \(x \to \infty\text{,}\) \(f'(x) \to 0\text{,}\) the slope of the tangents becomes less steep.
