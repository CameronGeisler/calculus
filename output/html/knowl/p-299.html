Let \(f\) be a function, continuous on \([a,b]\text{,}\) differentiable on \((a,b)\text{.}\) Then, there exists \(c \in (a,b)\) such that,

\begin{equation*} \boxed{f'(c) = \frac{f(b) - f(a)}{b - a}} \end{equation*}

or, equivalently, such that,

\begin{equation*} f(b) - f(a) = f'(c)(b - a) \end{equation*}
